## How we accelerated data loading into {{ ydb-short-name }} by 40 times

{% include notitle [practice_tag](../_includes/tags.md#practice) %}

! [](./_assets/2025/data-loading.jpg =yfmvar-1-yfmvarend)

The [article](https://habr.com/ru/companies/flocktory/articles/946454/) examines a real case of optimizing YDB performance, used as a storage for an ML Online Feature Store. The author focuses on practical methods for accelerating bulk data insertion and eliminating its impact on read speed.

The material is intended for professionals who need to organize fast loading of large volumes of data into YDB in high-load systems that simultaneously require low read latency. ## Sharded does not mean distributed: what you need to know when PostgreSQL is not enough

{% include notitle [practice_tag](../_includes/tags.md#practice) %}

! [](./_assets/2025/shards.jpg =yfmvar-3-yfmvarend)

This [article](https://habr.com/ru/companies/ydb/articles/907024/) discusses the organization of distributed transactions, data sharding, and the use of two-phase commit. Special attention is paid to an important aspect: two-phase commit only ensures atomicity of operations but does not guarantee transaction isolation, which can create certain expectations among developers.

The material is intended for professionals who need to scale systems beyond the capabilities of a single database (e.g., PostgreSQL) and transition to an architecture with data sharding. The article provides practical recommendations for choosing architectural solutions and explains key concepts of distributed transactions.
