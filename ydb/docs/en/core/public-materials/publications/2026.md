## A Billion Records and March 8: How YDB Saved the Holiday

{% include notitle [practice_tag](../_includes/tags.md#practice) %}

! [](./_assets/2026/milliard-ydb.jpeg =yfmvar-0-yfmvarend)

The [article](https://habr.com/ru/companies/yandex/articles/1014784/) examines the process of reworking the synchronization system for warehouse balances and prices in Yandex Food retail: from about a billion rows at the project start to several billion records of prices and stocks in production, and the transition from a PostgreSQL cache with periodic heavy JSON exports from partners to a distributed YDB storage with message broker topics, PATCH/SAVE commands, and protobuf in a data column. The author shows why full JSON snapshots and overwriting large fragments in the database stopped scaling, and how they were replaced with a change stream and deltas: why protobuf was chosen instead of wide tables and JSON, how to batch messages for the broker, how patch application works, and what data freshness requirements the data mart, search, and operational scenarios such as holiday orders demand.

The article will be useful for professionals who design or operate high-load systems with large data volumes, and who also face migration from a monolith and heavy SQL/JSON solutions to distributed storage such as YDB. The material shows a comparison of the "before" and "after" approaches and provides justification for key engineering decisions.
